# Accessibility

Streaming can be made more welcoming with a few practical choices.

## Captions

- Use automatic captions where available.
- Consider recording the stream and adding improved captions later.
- Speak clearly and avoid talking over collaborators.

## Visual clarity

- Use large fonts when screen-sharing code or slides.
- Prefer high-contrast themes.
- Avoid relying on colour alone to communicate meaning.
- Describe what you are doing when demonstrating visual interfaces.

## Chat and interaction

- Read out or summarise audience questions before answering.
- Give people multiple ways to participate, for example chat, Q&A documents or follow-up issues.
- Make expectations for behaviour clear.

## Recordings and notes

- Where possible, share links, commands, notes or transcripts after the stream.
- Consider whether a written summary would help people who could not attend live.
